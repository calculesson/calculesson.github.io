3rd grade

Several units lead to high school math.
It is important to review all the units you learned in junior high school.

What You Can Learn Here

  • Factorization

    Put simply, factorization is the process of taking an addition or subtraction expression and turning it into a multiplication expression.
    You can say it is the opposite of formula expansion.
    When Do You Use It?
    It is one way to solve the quadratic equation, which you will learn in a later unit.
    Also, you can use factorization to devise a calculation.
    ex) 101×101-99×99.

  • Square Root

    The square root of a is a number that becomes a when you square it.
    When Do You Use It?
    Maybe you imagine a square root as the length of one side of a square.
    Also, A4 and B5 paper is made so that the aspect ratio is 1:√2.

  • Quadratic Equations

    An equation whose highest degree is 2.
    It is expressed in the form ax2+bx+c=0
    When Do You Use It?
    There are many numbers that you can't find only with the linear equations learned in the 1st grade of junior high school.
    If you combine them with the "function y=ax2" learned in a later unit and the quadratic function learned in high school math I, you can find more numbers.

  • Function y=ax2

    It is a function where y is proportional to xsquared.
    The graph of this function is a curve called a parabola.
    When Do You Use It?
    A parabola represents the motion of a thrown object.
    They are also used in parabolic antennas that receive radio waves.
    By studying this unit, you can understand the basics of quadratic functions that you will learn in high school.
