1st grade

The name changes from arithmetic to math, and you probably make the mistake of saying "arithmetic is..." many times.

What You Can Learn Here

  • Positive/Negative Numbers

    Positive Numbers: greater than 0. Negative Numbers: less than 0.
    This is the first unit that students learn about numbers less than 0.
    When Do You Use It?
    ex) when you say a low temperature like "-5 °C"
    In addition, "-50% discount" means that it is more expensive than the original price.

  • Four Operations with Positive & Negative Numbers

    You will do the four operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) by using positive and negative numbers.

  • Literal Equation

    You represent numbers by replacing them with letters such as a or x.
    When Do You Use It?
    You can make an equation even if you don't know what number will go into it.
    ex) when you want to know how much money you need. You let the price for one pencil be x and express the price for four pencils as 4 x (yen).
    An advantage of using letters is that it takes less time to write explanations and equations.

  • Linear Equations in One Variable

    It is the equation that the degree of a polynomial is 1. The degree of a polinomial is the largest sum of the exponents of all variables in a term, so you don't use xy or x2 in this unit.
    When Do You Use It?
    You can find the numbers you want to know about your daily life by making and solving an equation.
    ex) when you want to know when to leave your home. You can find the best answer while considering the walking speed or the time you want to arrive.
